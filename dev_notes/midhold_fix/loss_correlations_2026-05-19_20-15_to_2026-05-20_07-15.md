## Aggregated insight

The **dominant loss pattern** is **direction × leverage × re-entry**:
- Brain emitted Sells in a bullish market (C1)
- Most trades were 5× leverage (C2)
- Brain re-entered losing symbols 11 min after losing (C3)
- Multiple trades from the same CALL_A batch lost together (C6)
- Concentrated in 03-06 UTC Asian session (C5)

The **mid-hold fix's INVALIDATED state lane is correct but slow** — 94% of losses closed before the fix's strongest signal could fire (C11). Most losses came via the existing SL or wd_claude_action paths.

The **most impactful operator-level tuning** would be:
1. Tighten J6 reentry-cooldown for symbols that just lost (raise from 10 min → 30-60 min) — reduces C3 trap
2. Cap brain's default leverage at 3× during high-volatility windows — mitigates C2 amplification
3. Add session-aware sizing throttle for 04-06 UTC — mitigates C5 concentration
4. Surface "recent loss on this symbol" prominently in CALL_A so brain weighs re-entry more carefully — reduces C3 / C6

The **mid-hold fix is doing what it can**, but the loss profile in this window was overwhelmingly explained by direction × leverage × re-entry — not by mid-hold blindness. The fix's marquee design intent (catch mid-hold thesis breaks before SL) only fires on slow-moving adverse moves, not the fast SL-hit losses that dominated this window.
