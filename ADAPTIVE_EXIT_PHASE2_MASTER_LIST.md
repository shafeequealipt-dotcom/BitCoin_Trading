# Adaptive Exit — Phase 2 Master List: The Twelve Proven Issues Plus Every Phase 1 Addition

This is the Phase 2 deliverable required by IMPLEMENT_DYNAMIC_ADAPTIVE_EXIT_SYSTEM.md: one de-duplicated, prioritized master list that merges the twelve proven issues from EXIT_SYSTEMS_DEEP_FORENSIC_FINDINGS.md with every additional hardcoded value, gap, anomaly, or mistuned threshold that the Phase 1 inventory surfaced. It is investigation and synthesis only; no code, configuration, or flag has been changed. It is written in prose with heading structure for screen-reader access: no tables, no emoji, no decorative separators. Each item carries its code location, its current behavior, the evidence it is wrong or should be adaptive, and the proposed adaptive basis.

## Method and de-duplication

The master list is built by taking the twelve proven forensic issues as the spine, attaching to each the underlying Phase 1 values that produce it (so a value and the forensic issue it causes are linked, not double-counted), and then appending the items Phase 1 found that the twelve did not name. The Phase 1 inventory found 526 hardcoded values; 193 are move-size or geometry values that should become adaptive, 312 are behavioral invariants confirmed to stay fixed and are not fix items here, and 21 are uncertain. Of the 193 plus 21 fixable values, 136 carry a forensic code and roll up under the twelve issues below; 78 carry no forensic code and are the additional findings, grouped into items M11 through M16. Full per-value detail lives in ADAPTIVE_EXIT_PHASE1_INVENTORY.md; this list references it rather than repeating all 526 entries.

## How priority is assigned

Priority follows the proven cost ranking in the forensic report, which is grounded in the give-back and fee numbers from the real trades. Tier 1 is the move-size-to-toolset mismatch, the root cost that makes the trail dead, the ladder stuck at a sliver, and the take-profit unreachable; every clipped winner traces here. Tier 2 is the gateway clamp-noop, which suppresses the only working profit lock even within the current calibration, so fixing Tier 1 without Tier 2 would still leave the lock unable to write. Tier 3 is the dead-drifter gap. Tier 4 is the faded-winner lockout and the unactivated recovery capture. Tier 5 is the wrong-side wire-fails and the hardcoded hard stop. Tier 6 is the additional Phase 1 findings beyond the twelve, sequenced after the core because they are mostly loss-side room, advisory triggers, and validation bounds whose cost is smaller and partly conditional.

## Tier 1 — The move-size-to-toolset mismatch (the root cost)

### M1 — The ladder arm and rungs are unreachable, so only a breakeven sliver is ever locked

Forensic codes F2, C1, G1. Code location: the stepped break-even ladder in _compute_ladder_floor at src/workers/profit_sniper.py around lines 1941 to 2153, reading min_profit_to_arm_ladder_pct 0.2 percent (config.toml line 2015), ladder_step_pct_young 0.6 percent and old 0.4 percent (lines 1996 and 1997), lock_offset_pct_young 0.3 percent and old 0.2 percent (lines 2000 and 2001), and ladder_breakeven_lock_pct 0.05 percent (line 2031). Current behavior: the first real rung sits at 0.6 percent; with a median peak of 0.23 percent and a ninetieth percentile of 0.71 percent, about 97.6 percent of graduated trades never cross even the first rung, so the stepped lock produces level zero and only the zero-crossing breakeven floor fires, locking a 0.05 to 0.13 percent sliver. Evidence: 97.6 percent of 126 graduated trades peaked inside the dead band; median written lock 0.050 percent across three days. Proposed adaptive basis: the arm becomes the maximum of 0.5R and the fee floor; the rungs become 1.5R, 3R, and 5R; the lock offsets and breakeven lock become bounded fractions of R floored at the round-trip fee, so the first rung is reachable within a typical move and the lock is net-positive. Target: Commit 2 (with the fee floor and coefficients landed in Commit 0).

### M2 — The Chandelier trail can never win, because its minimum distance exceeds the whole move

Forensic codes F1, C2. Code location: _compute_trail_stop at src/workers/profit_sniper.py around lines 1790 to 1939, reading the trail distance as base_atr_mult times ATR times regime, profit-decay, and momentum factors (the profit-fetching path uses atr_multiple young 3.0 and old 1.0; the legacy base is base_atr_multiplier 2.5 at config.toml line 1747), with a hard minimum of the maximum of min_trail_atr_multiplier 1.5 times ATR and min_trail_pct 0.30 percent of entry (config.toml lines 1878 and 1879), and a breakeven floor at entry around lines 1893 to 1894. The from-current-price floor bounds (trail_floor_from_price min 0.20 and max 1.50 percent) and trail_min_change_pct 0.1 percent compound it. Current behavior: the 0.30 percent minimum distance alone exceeds the median 0.23 percent peak, so the trail stop falls below entry, the breakeven floor pulls it to entry, and under highest-stop-wins the ladder's entry-plus-a-sliver always beats it. Evidence: the trail won 8 of 3,319 spine selections over three days and zero in the clean hour. Proposed adaptive basis: the trail distance becomes 1R behind the running peak, and the minimum-distance floor is lowered so 1R on a quiet coin (for example 0.12 percent) is allowed rather than clamped back to 0.30 percent, floored at the round-trip fee. The regime and momentum factors stay as dimensionless multipliers on the R-based distance. Target: Commit 2.

### M3 — The take-profit and the score-engine profit gates are unreachable by construction

Forensic codes F3, C3. Code location: default_take_profit_pct 6.0 percent (config.toml line 634) and the several min_rr definitions (min_rr_ratio 1.1 at line 1131, 2.0 at line 2692, level_2_min_rr 3.0 at line 1720; the stop_loss recommend min R:R 1.5 at src/risk/stop_loss.py lines 142 and 148); the flip take-profit capper bounds in src/core/flip_tp_capper.py; the volatility-profile tp bounds (config.toml lines 2950 to 2953); and the score-engine profit gates min_profit_for_close 0.50 percent (default settings.py line 2607, used profit_sniper.py around 3733) and min_profit_pct 0.80 percent (default settings.py line 2605, used around 4379), plus the anti-greed pullback backstops requiring 2, 3, and 5 percent peaks (profit_sniper.py lines 3769, 3772, 3775). Current behavior: the nearest possible take-profit sits near 2.25 percent against a ninetieth-percentile peak of 0.71 percent; the score engine downgrades every full-close and every actionable classification because real winners never reach 0.50 or 0.80 percent; the anti-greed backstops are dead code for the real distribution. Evidence: zero take-profit closes across 257 trades on the full day and across the multi-day window. Proposed adaptive basis: the take-profit becomes a reachable R-multiple, for example 4R to 6R; the score-engine profit gates become R-multiples floored at the fee so they are reachable for sub-0.3-percent movers; the anti-greed peak thresholds become R-multiples so they scale to the coin. The multiple min_rr definitions are reconciled to the correct one for each consumer. Target: Commit 2, with the score-engine gate magnitudes confirmed in the Phase 3 design.

### M4 — No profit threshold clears fees, so locked wins are erased by cost

Forensic code C5. Code location: the fee-aware machinery already partly present — micro_floor_arm_pct 0.10 percent (config.toml line 2060), micro_floor_arm_fee_aware_enabled true (line 2078), ladder_lock_fee_clearance_pct 0.13 percent (line 2047), min_profit_for_action 0.10 percent and min_profit_for_partial_pct 0.0 percent — measured against the round-trip taker fee of 0.11 percent (cap_round_trip_fee_pct at config.toml line 2212; _BYBIT_TAKER_FEE_PER_SIDE 0.00055 at src/core/trade_coordinator.py line 29). Current behavior: ladder_breakeven_lock_pct 0.05 percent and micro_floor_arm_pct 0.10 percent both sit below the 0.11 percent round-trip fee, so a breakeven lock books a guaranteed net fee loss; the fee-aware lift to 0.13 percent only acts when the peak clears that hurdle, and the fee-aware suppression leaves peaks between 0.10 and 0.13 percent entirely unprotected (524 events). Evidence: 524 MICRO_FLOOR_FEE_SUPPRESS events; median written lock 0.050 percent, below fee. Proposed adaptive basis: every profit threshold (arm, locks, take-profit) is the larger of its R-multiple and the fee floor, where the fee floor is the round-trip cost times a small safety buffer, drawn from one canonical fee constant rather than scattered literals. Target: the fee floor and canonical constant land in Commit 0; consumers adopt it in Commit 2.

## Tier 2 — The gateway clamp-noop (the enabler that must ship first)

### M5 — The gateway silently drops the only working profit lock

Forensic codes B1, C4. Code location: src/core/sl_gateway.py, the R2 minimum-distance path lines 694 to 815 (effective minimum from vol_scale.min_distance_for_class, lines 706 to 716), the existing breakeven carve-out lines 743 to 815, and the final tighten-only no-op re-check lines 898 to 910 (REASON_CLAMP_NOOP); mirrored in the sniper pre-screen at profit_sniper.py around lines 3296 to 3349. Current behavior: when the clamped lock does not improve on the current stop it is rejected as a no-op and nothing is written; the existing carve-out holds the stop only down to breakeven, which does not improve on a stop already at breakeven, so the lock is dropped. Evidence: 2,193 of 2,201 gateway rejects over three days (99.6 percent) were clamp-noops; the ALICEUSDT trace held a 0.16 percent peak at only 0.05 percent. Correction from Phase 1: the binding cause is the no-op re-check plus the breakeven-only hold, not the dead-class ceiling re-imposing 0.30 percent (the effective minimum on a dead coin is about 0.05 percent). Proposed adaptive basis: extend the trusted-source carve-out so an armed adaptive profit lock holds its R-derived, fee-floored value (above breakeven), which does improve and therefore writes, while tighten-only, the wrong-side guard, the maximum-step rule, and the rate-limit remain unchanged and the catastrophic precedence is untouched. This must ship before M1 and M2 or the rescaled locks will be clamped away. Target: Commit 1.

## Tier 3 — The dead-drifter band

### M6 — A flat drifter is neither scratched nor cut, tying up capital to the deadline

Forensic codes F4, G2, C6. Code location: the development guard in src/risk/layer4_protection.py _check_profit lines 441 to 471 and its mirror in the sniper stall valve at profit_sniper.py around lines 4617 to 4645, reading profit_protection_threshold 0.0 percent (settings.py line 2706) and development_window_lower minus 0.3 percent (settings.py line 2707, config.toml line 1935); the dead-band give-back ladder_deadband_giveback_pct 0.10 percent (config.toml line 2037). Current behavior: a trade between minus 0.3 and 0.0 percent is above the development floor so it cannot be stall-cut, below breakeven so the profit tools do not own it, and not deep enough to trip any loss model, so it rides until the stall timeout near the deadline. Evidence: LINKUSDT held 2,637 seconds with 383 consecutive development-guard blocks. Proposed adaptive basis: the development floor becomes R-relative, and a trade whose lifetime peak never reached 1R and that is old enough to be judged dead releases the guard and triggers a scratch-exit (take the small net gain if above the fee floor, else close flat), riding the existing guard and close path without a new gate, and never bypassing the existing late-bloomer veto. The age discriminator and the requirement not to bypass the veto are escalation points for the design gate. Target: Commit 4.

## Tier 4 — The faded winner and the recovered fighter

### M7 — A graduated winner that craters is locked out of the loss tools

Forensic code F5. Code location: the owner gate faded-winner rule in src/core/sl_gateway.py around lines 1295 to 1303, reading faded_winner_rearm_red false (config.toml line 1010) and graduation_crater_rearm_enabled false (config.toml line 2183, with graduation_crater_loss_pct 0.5 at line 2184); the peak-protection extension peak_protection_threshold_pct 0.10 percent (settings.py line 2723). Current behavior: a once-green trade stays green-owned even when it craters, so it is managed only by breakeven-level profit tools and the catastrophic cap, with the loss-cutting structure and recovery locked out. Evidence: the offer_loss equal to False field on green-owned spine lines. Proposed adaptive basis: the program does not change ownership (the owner hierarchy is unchanged), so this is handled by the geometry being live and R-relative — the faded winner's protective floor and the crater re-arm threshold become R-derived and fee-floored, and the design states explicitly whether the crater re-arm should be enabled, which is an escalation point. Target: Commit 5 (verification-led).

### M8 — A recovered trade's bounce is detected but rarely captured

Forensic code G3. Code location: the final-phase recovery candidate _lc_recovery_candidate at profit_sniper.py around lines 2246 to 2313 (recovery_bounce_trail_atr_profit_side 1.5 and loss_side 0.40, recovery_profit_side_ratio, recovery_final_fraction) and the time-decay recovery-tighten at src/risk/time_decay_sl.py around lines 903 to 920 (mae_tightening_recovery_threshold, recovery_tightening_buffer_pct 0.3 percent); the sniper recovering_threshold_pct 0.10 percent (settings.py line 2734). Evidence: ATOMUSDT recovered to minus 0.081 percent then closed by win-probability, not the recovery stop. Proposed adaptive basis: the recovery trail distances and the recovery-tighten buffer become bounded fractions of R floored at the fee, so the capture stop sits inside the coin's move and clears the gateway once M5 is in place. Target: Commit 5.

## Tier 5 — The hard stop, the initial stop, and the wire-fail

### M9 — The hard stop is a hardcoded minus 3 percent literal, not volatility-scaled

Forensic code E1. Code location: the inline literal if pnl_pct is less than minus 3.0 at src/workers/position_watchdog.py line 2596; related loss-room values the initial ATR stop atr_initial_multiple_young 3.0 and old 1.0 (config.toml lines 2231 and 2232, used at profit_sniper.py around line 1273) and the naked safety_stop_pct 2.5 percent (config.toml line 2127). Current behavior: minus 3 percent is far too loose for a quiet coin and would be too tight for a very volatile one; it is inert at current move sizes but contradicts the configuration-driven design. Evidence: no trade reached it in any window. Proposed adaptive basis: the hard stop becomes a wide R-multiple, for example 8R to 10R, always floored below the sacred catastrophic cap, which stays sacred and only tightens; the initial ATR stop uses the same R. The forced catastrophic-stop test gates this, and any R path that could place the hard stop above the cap is an escalation point. Target: Commit 3.

### M10 — The wrong-side wire-fail is a calibration artifact mislabeled as an outage

Forensic codes B2, A1. Code location: the adapter wrong-side rejection in src/bybit_demo around the set-stop path, surfaced as a wire-fail in src/core/sl_gateway.py around lines 996 to 1019, raised to the event buffer at HIGH severity while logged at warning (the A1 mismatch). Current behavior: a profit-lock stop computed so close to the mark that a single tick puts it on the wrong side is refused by the adapter and surfaced as if downstream were broken. Evidence: all 162 wire-fails over three days came from the profit-lock path and coincide with wrong-side adapter rejections, not transport errors. Proposed adaptive basis: this resolves as a side effect of M1, M2, and M5, because locks sized to the coin's real movement clear the minimum distance and stop landing on the wrong side; separately, the event severity is reclassified so a calibration artifact does not page the operator as an outage. Target: resolves with Commits 1 and 2; the severity reclassification is a small change sequenced in the Phase 3 design.

## Tier 6 — Additional Phase 1 findings beyond the twelve

## Mapping the master list to the ship sequence

Commit 0 lands the centralized adaptive configuration and the fee floor and the pure R-geometry functions, with no behavior change, supporting M1 through M9. Commit 1 fixes M5, the gateway profit-lock exemption, and must enforce before the rescaling. Commit 2 fixes M1, M2, M3, and M4, the ladder, trail, take-profit, and fee floor adoption, and dissolves M10's wire-fails as a side effect. Commit 3 fixes M9, the hard stop and initial stop, with the forced catastrophic-stop test. Commit 4 fixes M6, the dead-drifter scratch. Commit 5 verifies and aligns M7 and M8, the faded winner and recovery capture. The Tier 6 items M11 through M16 are sequenced in the Phase 3 design after the core, with M12 and the scope of M15 raised as escalation points, because they are loss-side room, advisory triggers, validation bounds, and correctness fixes whose cost is smaller and partly conditional.

## Summary

The master list contains the twelve proven forensic issues as items M1 through M10 (the move-size mismatch split across the ladder, trail, take-profit, and fee floor, then the clamp-noop, the dead-drifter, the faded winner, the recovery capture, the hard stop, and the wire-fail), plus six additional items M11 through M16 that Phase 1 surfaced beyond the twelve (the loss-engine room scalers, the uncertain behavioral-gate magnitudes, the watchdog advisory triggers, the sentinel deadline tiers, the validator and default-stop bounds, and the correctness items). It is de-duplicated by linking each value to the issue it causes, and prioritized by the proven cost. No code has been changed. This master list awaits operator approval before the Phase 3 design.
